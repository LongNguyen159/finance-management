User Manual for Easy Sankey

Easily Visualise Your Financial Data

Why Easy Sankey?

Easy Sankey helps you create clear and simple financial visualisations without the need for Excel or technical skills. Just a few easy inputs, and you can see where your money goes!

Getting Started

On the main page, click the "Edit Input" button in the top-right corner. This will open a menu where you can enter your financial information.

Input Fields Explained

Hereâ€™s a quick overview to help you fill in the input fields:

  • Type: Choose the type of entry:
    • Income: Your earnings.
    • Expense: Your spending.
    • Tax: Your tax amount. You can only enter this once.
  • Name: Give your entry a name (e.g., "Rent" or "Groceries").
  • Amount: Enter the amount of money (e.g., "800" for rent).
  • Part of (optional): This field lets you break down expenses.
    • Example: For "Housing," you can specify that it includes "Rent" and "Wifi," both of which are part of "Housing."
    • Default behavior: If no "Part of" is specified for an expense, it will automatically be linked to your income.
    • "Default income" option: Choose this option if you want to remove the grouping.

    • ðŸ’¡ Tips:

    • "Part of" can be used to group expenses together!
    • If you are unsure what is the total value of your expenses, just define a group with initial value of 0. The app will calculate the total value of the group for you as you add more items into it.
    • Example: You define a group called "Housing" with initial value of 0. You add "Rent" with value of 800 and "Wifi" with value of 50, then specify them as 'Part of' "Housing". The app will automatically calculate the total value of "Housing" as 850.

What Happens Next?

As you add entries:

  • If you enter more than one income, Easy Sankey will calculate your total income.
  • If you enter a tax amount, the app will show your "usable income" after taxes are deducted.

Note: Avoid entering multiple entries with the same name to ensure accurate expense calculations.
